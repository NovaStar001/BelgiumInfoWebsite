Andrew Goodman, James Chaney, and Michael Schwerner were three civil rights workers, who were murdered by members of the KKK. Before their deaths, they had all contributed to the Freedom Summer movement. Even in death, they managed to assist the movement, by drawing attention to the crimes committed in Mississippi.

The Freedom Summer movement was intended to educate African American citizens, so they would be able to pass the, altered, voter registration process. The volunteers educated 17,000 individuals, while only 1,600 managed to register to vote. Additionally, 30 Freedom Schools were setup by the end of the movement.
