Steve Jobs

Obstacles

One of the biggest obstacles with starting Apple was the efficiency of their engineers. The problem wasn't that their engineers weren't working fast enough, but rather, they were working too fast. The engineers were making so much progress but on different projects. This meant that Steve Jobs had to end some great ideas, to ensure that other projects would flourish.

Another issue Apple faced was funding. Computer hardware wasn't as easy to come by in the 70s and 80s, meaning Apple had to spend large amounts of money on small amounts of hardware. By the '80s, Apple was on the brink of bankruptcy, until Bill Gates donated $150 million. It wasn't until 2007, that Bill Gates revealed his motive for helping a competitor. He revealed that he was investing in a future business opportunity, which, in 2023, seems to have worked out fairly well.
