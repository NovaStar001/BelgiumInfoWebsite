Belgium

Cultural Information

  • Religion

    • According to the Belgian constitution, citizens have rights to freedom of relgion. Christianity is the largest religion in Belgium, with 65% of the population.

    Catholicism is the largest branch of Christianity, not just in Belgium, making up 58% of the Christian population.

    About 5% of the population is Muslim, while 25% are Agnostic / Atheist.

  • Government

    • Belgium's government operates on a Federal Parliamentary Democracy, under a Constitutional Monarchy. They also have a multi-party system, with 7 main parties

    and many minor parties. The major parties are mainly seperated by the citizens' origin. While the minor parties tend to pair up with other parties, to make their

    collective audience bigger.

  • People

    • The people of Belgium are refered to as Belgians. Although, the variety in culture throughout Belgiums history creates other terms for some groups of people, such as

    the Flemish people and the Walloons. The three most common jobs include office work, service, high-tech industries. Among those jobs, the most demanded skills are

    Cybersecurity Experts, Healthcare Professionals, and Data Scientists

  • Accomplishments

    • Belgium's first major accomplishment, as is for many countries, was gaining independence from their ruling nation, which was the Northern Netherlands in the case of

    Belgium. Belgium also helped form the United Nations, in 1945. Similarly, Belgium was a founding member of the World Health Organization. Belgium has also served 6

    membershsips on the UNSC, with a planned 7th membership.
