  • Resolution on Combating Inhumane Treatment of Prisoners and Addressing Hate Crimes
  • Against the LGBT Community
  • RECALLING the principles and purposes of the Charter of the United Nations, including the
  • commitment to promoting and encouraging respect for human rights and fundamental freedoms
  • for all without distinction as to race, sex, language, or religion,
  • REAFFIRMING the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, which states that all human beings
  • are born free and equal in dignity and rights,
  • EMPHASIZING the importance of the United Nations Standard Minimum Rules for the
  • Treatment of Prisoners (the Nelson Mandela Rules) and the Yogyakarta Principles on the
  • Application of International Human Rights Law in relation to Sexual Orientation and Gender
  • Identity,
  • RECOGNIZING that all individuals, regardless of their sexual orientation, gender identity, or
  • gender expression, should be treated with dignity and respect and protected from discrimination
  • and violence,
  • DEEPLY CONCERNED by reports of inhumane treatment of prisoners, including but not limited
  • to torture, ill-treatment, and degrading conditions of detention, as well as the prevalence of hate
  • crimes against individuals based on their real or perceived sexual orientation, gender identity, or
  • gender expression,
  • EXPRESSING GRAVE CONCERN over the disproportionate impact of these violations on the
  • lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, and intersex (LGBTI) community, particularly those who are
  • marginalized and vulnerable,
  • REITERATING the obligation of all member states to ensure the full and effective protection of
  • the human rights of all individuals within their jurisdiction, regardless of their sexual orientation,
  • gender identity, or gender expression,
  • BE IT RESOLVED
  • THAT all forms of inhumane treatment of prisoners, including
  • torture, ill-treatment, and degrading conditions of detention, and calls upon all member
  • states to adhere to and implement the United Nations Standard Minimum Rules for the
  • Treatment of Prisoners (the Nelson Mandela Rules) to prevent such abuses;
  • THAT hate crimes and acts of violence committed against individuals based on their real
  • or perceived sexual orientation, gender identity, or gender expression, and urges
  • member states to take effective measures to prevent and combat hate crimes, including
  • through the enactment and enforcement of appropriate legislation;
  • THAT member states should collect and maintain data on hate crimes based on sexual
  • orientation, gender identity, or gender expression, and share such data with relevant
  • international and regional organizations, in order to develop effective strategies for
  • preventing and addressing hate crimes;
  • THAT member states ensure that prisoners who are LGBTI are provided with safe and
  • respectful conditions of detention, access to appropriate medical care, and protection
  • from discrimination, harassment, and violence;
  • THAT member states cooperate with relevant United Nations bodies, international
  • organizations, civil society organizations, and the LGBTI community in efforts to address
  • the issues outlined in this resolution, including by sharing best practices and experiences
  • in preventing and addressing hate crimes and inhumane treatment of prisoners;
  • THAT the United Nations High Commissioner for Human Rights continues to monitor
  • and report on human rights violations against individuals based on their sexual
  • orientation, gender identity, or gender expression, and to provide technical assistance to
  • member states in implementing measures to prevent and address such violations;
