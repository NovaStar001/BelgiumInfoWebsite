Dissections can be extremely crucial pieces of information. Whether its to learn about the internal structure of animals, or to determine the cause of death of a person. Without dissections we wouldn't have some crucial pieces of evidence for evolution, which might have led biological science down a very different path. Dissections have shown us that most, if not every, animal shares the same organs and basic internal structure.

The first recorded human dissection was in Alexandria, a polis of ancient Greece, around 335 B.C. However, these dissections were soon halted, due to common belief that an unintact body would be kept out of the afterlife. It is believed that the reason for this dissection was simply to learn about human anatomy.
