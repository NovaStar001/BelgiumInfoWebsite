Belfry of Bruges

Belfry of Bruges is a medieval bell tower. As the name suggests, it is located in the center of Bruges. The tower stood from 1240 to 1741, where it fell victim to a fire.

A gothic style spire was added to the rooftop in 1822. Standing at 272 feet tall, Belfry of Bruges is the tallest bell tower in Belgium.

Grand Place

Grand Place is a collection of buildings, all of which share the same 17th century architecture. Today, it is home to the Brussels Town Hall, and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

In the 17th century, Grand Place was used as a standard market, where citizens sold and bought goods.
